The idea

A shelf of one-tap strategies on our own AMM.

The same "select a strategy, put in config, hit play" surface the team sketched, living inside the Doma app, not a months-long standalone bot platform. The user authorizes once; every strategy is just intents on our Uniswap V3 pools, executed by the limit-order backend. The screen stays a thin layer: configure and watch profit-and-loss. The trading itself runs server-side.

The flow

Four steps, whichever strategy you pick.

Every strategy runs through the same four-step path; only the configure step changes shape.

01

Choose a strategy

A gallery of playbooks, Grid trading, Buy the floor discount, Buy low / sell high, and DCA, each with a plain-English one-liner and a tag.

02

Configure, with the orders drawn on the chart

Each strategy has its own configure screen with a live price chart, validation, and a fee-aware profit line. Grid has Auto (a suggested range from recent volatility) and Manual (every field, with error states for an inverted range, a too-small order, or profit below fees). The others collapse to their own handful of fields.

03

Authorize, once, and capped

One approval lets the agent trade on your behalf, but only on Doma pairs and only up to the cap you set. One approval covers every strategy; a new strategy inside your unallocated approval needs no signature. Pause or revoke anytime.

04

Monitor, realized vs floating, live

Realized grid profit sits next to unrealized P&L, with executed fills in the activity feed. Pause or close a strategy (sell back to USDC or keep the tokens), read the matched-trades history, and handle out-of-range and budget-used states. Run several at once from a "My strategies" dashboard.

What this needs from the infra

Most of the shelf ships on the limit-order infra as-is. A few pieces need one more building block.

The split is about execution actions, not triggers: everything price- or schedule-based already fits.

Ready on the limit-order infra

Grid trading (one pass)

A ladder of resting buy/sell limit orders placed across your range.

Buy the floor discount

One conditional buy at the on-chain buyout floor times one minus your discount. The Doma-exclusive strategy, and it needs nothing new.

Buy low, sell high (run once)

A one-level grid, buy at your low, sell at your high, then stop. Inder's "combo of limit orders," verbatim.

DCA (single buy)

One scheduled buy, already working in the current POC.

Needs one more building block

Repeating (grid loop / repeat)

To keep cycling it must re-arm the opposite order on each fill, requires re-arm-on-fill chaining in the orders-worker, not the browser.

DCA scheduling

The engine is price-triggered, requires a time trigger to run buys on a clock.

Buy during bonding

Trigger fits, but a pre-graduation token has no V3 pool yet, requires a bonding-curve fill path alongside the swap-based fill.

Buy & create LP / LP-mode grid

The "create LP" half is a mint, not a swap, requires an LP-provisioning action chained after the fill.

Limit orders vs liquidity

Why not just provide liquidity?

A narrow Uniswap V3 band above or below price is a range order, so one grid level looks like one LP band. The products still differ where it counts.

Fills
A filled limit order stays filled. An LP band converts back if price re-crosses it, so locking a fill would need a bot to withdraw the position.
Fees
Through the infra you are the taker and pay the pool fee plus slippage per fill. As an LP you are the maker and earn it. That is why the configure screen is fee-aware.
Control
A limit order has an exact trigger, slippage protection, and a stop loss. An LP band below price buys the falling token unconditionally and cannot stop.
Coverage
Conditional orders work on bonding-curve tokens with no pool yet. LP needs a live pool.

The compounding future version places each grid level as a narrow LP band and withdraws it on the cross, so the grid earns fees per cycle instead of paying them. That depends on LP-position primitives from the infra team, an open question below.

Under the hood

Why it's a thin frontend on top of what we're building.

Trigger
The limit-order backend already watches Uniswap swaps and fills resting orders. A grid is just a set of those orders that re-arms on fill.
Where it runs
The hot loop lives on the server (orders-worker), so it keeps running when the tab is closed. The browser only configures and displays.
Extra infra
None for grid / buy-low, as long as the re-arm-on-fill is a first-class primitive in the orders-worker rather than a browser loop.
Fairness
A grid's orders spread across many price levels, so the "who fills first" problem mostly dissolves. v1 is first-come-first-serve with disclosures, per the team's call.

Open questions for the limit-order team

What the infra needs to expose for strategies to sit on top.

Grouping
Orders filter by wallet address, but nothing says "these belong to this grid." A grid needs a strategy id on the order so the UI can show, cancel, and account for it as a unit.
Re-arm
Is chaining (place the opposite order when one fills) a first-class primitive in the orders-worker, or does the strategy layer poll and re-place?
Per-pair FIFO
One order per pair at a time serializes a grid against itself and against other users. How does a many-order grid coexist with that guard?
LP primitives
Can the infra mint and withdraw a narrow range position on a trigger? That unlocks the fee-earning LP-mode grid and buy-and-create-LP.
Also
Fill notifications (push vs poll), realized-vs-unrealized P&L accounting, and batch cancel / expiry / revoke as a kill switch.
