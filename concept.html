The idea

A shelf of one-tap strategies on our own AMM.

The same "select a strategy, put in config, hit play" surface the team sketched, living inside the Doma app, not a months-long standalone bot platform. The user authorizes once; every strategy is just intents on our Uniswap V3 pools, executed by the limit-order backend. The screen stays a thin layer: configure and watch profit-and-loss. The trading itself runs server-side.

The flow

Four steps, whichever strategy you pick.

Grid trading and buy-low / sell-high both run through the same four-step path; only the configure step changes shape.

01

Choose a strategy

A small gallery of playbooks, Grid trading, Buy low / sell high, DCA, each with a plain-English one-liner and a "best for" tag.

02

Configure, with the orders drawn on the chart

Set the range, grid count, and amount. A live price chart shows exactly where each buy sits below price and each sell above it, so the strategy is legible before you commit. Buy-low / sell-high collapses this to a single buy price, sell price, and amount.

03

Authorize, once, and capped

One approval lets the agent trade on your behalf, but only on Doma pairs and only up to the cap you set. Pause or revoke anytime.

04

Monitor, realized vs floating, live

Grid profit (realized, from matched buy-low/sell-high pairs) sits next to unrealized P&L, with executed fills plotted on the grid. Run several at once from a "My strategies" view.

What this needs from the infra

Two strategies ship on the limit-order infra as-is. Two need one more building block.

The split is clean, and it's about execution actions, not triggers: everything price- or schedule-based already fits.

Ready on the limit-order infra

Grid trading

A ladder of resting buy/sell limit orders that re-arm the opposite side on each fill. The re-arm lives in the orders-worker; the screen just tracks the range.

Buy low, sell high

A one-level grid, buy at your low, sell at your high, repeat. Inder's "combo of limit orders," verbatim.

DCA on a timer

A scheduled buy, already working in the current POC.

Needs one more building block

Buy during bonding

Trigger fits fine, but a pre-graduation token has no V3 pool yet, requires a bonding-curve fill path alongside the swap-based fill.

Buy & create LP

The buy is a normal limit order; the "create LP" half is a mint, not a swap, requires an LP-provisioning action chained after the fill.

Under the hood

Why it's a thin frontend on top of what we're building.

Trigger
The limit-order backend already watches Uniswap swaps and fills resting orders. A grid is just a set of those orders that re-arms on fill.
Where it runs
The hot loop lives on the server (orders-worker), so it keeps running when the tab is closed. The browser only configures and displays.
Extra infra
None for grid / buy-low, as long as the re-arm-on-fill is a first-class primitive in the orders-worker rather than a browser loop.
Fairness
A grid's orders spread across many price levels, so the "who fills first" problem mostly dissolves. v1 is first-come-first-serve with disclosures, per the team's call.
